I've been feeling fatigued lately and suspect it might be related to my diet. Any recommendations for energy-boosting foods?

Urgency:

Thank you for your question about fatigue and diet. Diet can indeed significantly impact your energy levels. Here are some recommendations:


1. Focus on complex carbohydrates like whole grains, which release energy slowly throughout the day.

2. Include protein in each meal (lean meats, legumes, dairy) to stabilize blood sugar.

3. Stay hydrated - even mild dehydration can cause fatigue.

4. Consider iron-rich foods (leafy greens, lentils) as iron deficiency is a common cause of fatigue.

5. Include B-vitamin rich foods like eggs, nuts, and seeds.


I'd also recommend getting basic blood work done to rule out any deficiencies. Feel free to follow up if you have more questions!

Your Question:

I've hit a plateau in my weight loss journey. I've been following a calorie deficit diet for 3 months and lost 8kg, but haven't seen any change in the last 2 weeks. What should I do?

Urgency:

Medium

Submitted:

2025-05-10 09:15 AM IST

Dr. Rajesh Kumar

Weight Management Specialist

Responded: 2025-05-11 11:30 AM IST

Weight loss plateaus are completely normal and actually a sign that your body is adapting. Here are some strategies to overcome this plateau:


1. Reassess your calorie needs - as you lose weight, your body requires fewer calories.

2. Change your exercise routine - your body adapts to the same workout over time.

3. Focus on strength training to build muscle, which increases metabolism.

4. Ensure you're getting enough sleep and managing stress.

5. Track your food intake carefully - sometimes portion sizes creep up unnoticed.


Remember that weight fluctuations are normal, and two weeks isn't necessarily cause for concern. Focus on non-scale victories like how your clothes fit and your energy levels. Great job on losing 8kg so far!
